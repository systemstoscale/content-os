01 — The big picture

Tell it what to post.
It does the rest.

You give it a topic. It writes the post, designs the visual, picks the best caption for each platform, and ships it to your audience. You stay in your zone — making offers, talking to clients, building the business.

02 — Four ways to ask

Use the channel
that fits the moment.

On your laptop, send an email. On the move, tap your phone. Or set it up once and have it post for you every morning while you sleep.

The daily option uses your content pillars — the 5–7 themes you set up once. It never repeats the same theme twice in a week.

04 — Idea to live post

Six steps.
Two of them are you.

Most of the work happens without you. You spend ~30 seconds on the ask and another 10 seconds saying "ship." That's the whole job.

05 — Your accounts, your control

Nothing is rented.
Everything is yours.

The assistant lives inside your Cloudflare account. Your Instagram, LinkedIn, YouTube, etc. are connected with your tokens. If you ever stop using Acquisition OS, your accounts and content stay where they are.

06 — Automatic vs. needs you

Clear rules
about who decides.

Static posts (carousels, quotes) can run on autopilot. Anything with your face or voice — or anything that costs money — requires your green light. Every time. No exceptions.

Happens automatically Needs your green light
→Picking today's topic →Publishing a reel you filmed
→Writing the caption →Publishing an AI-avatar video
→Designing the visuals →Publishing a YouTube long-form
→Picking the right platforms →Setting Meta ad budgets
→Publishing daily carousels & quotes →Anything you flag as sensitive
Three independent safety gates sit between the assistant and your ad spend. Even if all three failed, your ad budget is still $0 until you change it manually.
07 — Why your stuff is safe

The assistant uses your keys.
It never sees them.

Your passwords for Instagram, YouTube, Meta, etc. are locked away in your Cloudflare vault. The assistant can use them to publish — but it never reads them, never sees them, never holds them. Even if someone tried to trick it into leaking secrets, there's nothing to leak.

flowchart TB
  YOU["You"]
  VAULT["Your locked vault
(platform passwords,
API keys)"]
  AI["Your AI assistant
(only sees task names,
never the keys)"]
  PLATFORMS["Your social platforms
+ Meta Ads Manager"]

  YOU -- "set passwords
once at setup" --> VAULT
  YOU -- "ask for content" --> AI
  AI -- "asks the vault
to publish on its behalf" --> VAULT
  VAULT -- "uses the keys
to post for you" --> PLATFORMS
The point: the assistant is a worker, not a key-holder. If you ever revoke a platform's access, the vault simply can't publish there anymore — nothing else changes.
